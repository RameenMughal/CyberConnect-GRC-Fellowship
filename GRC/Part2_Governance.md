## Practical Example

**Practical Example**: Suppose a company wants to migrate operations to the cloud, which is manage by third party provider. So how governance works here?

The company ABC has its own data center and now it wants to transfer it to a cloud service such as AWS which is a third party provider.

We need five things:
1. Strategy: Define how to move services into the cloud.
2. Policy: How our security will be in the cloud domain. We need cloud security policy.
3. Responsibility: Who will move the services and who will oversee these operations.
4. Risk: What can be the risk if we move the services to the cloud in third party cloud environment.
5. Controls: To check what data is basically moving in the cloud. What is the impact on the compliance with the legal regulated frameworks.

If we dont do any governance and directly move data into the cloud, we can face any risk such as data breach.

## How things wok in the Organization

- Understand the business requirements
- Build Information Security Strategy
- Draft Policy because it basically tells why we need this rule.
- Form the department = accountability and Responsibility Matrix
- Conduct Risk Assessment = identify gap
- Build Program Management or Program Chart
- Build Metrics: To measure the impact and effectiveness of something
- Measure Metrics for Compliance Assessment

An accountability and responsibility matrix (commonly a RACI chart) defines project roles—Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed—to clarify tasks and prevent ownership gaps.

It maps tasks to team members, ensuring only one person is Accountable (A) per task, while multiple people can be Responsible (R).

## Planning

<img width="875" height="486" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/e1ddd315-fa7d-465c-9b92-83833bd4d654" />

In order to make good Governance, we need planning:

1. **Strategic Planning**: Occur at the highest position of the organization and for long period of time like five or more years known as Information Security Strategic Plan,
2. **Tactical Planning**: Process of creating mid-term plans to implement the strategies defined in strategic planning. It focuses on how to achieve specific goals using available resources within a limited time period.
3. **Operational Planning**: Creating short-term detailed, day-to-day plans and procedures to carry out tactical plans. It focuses on specific tasks, schedules, and resources needed to run daily operations efficiently.
